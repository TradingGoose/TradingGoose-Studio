### Design Decisions
- Workspace credentials are first-class records, while OAuth `account` rows are token storage. Authorization belongs to `authorizeCredentialUse()` and `listOAuthCredentialsForUser()`, not direct account-row ownership checks.
- Trading account identity is the tuple `providerId + credentialId + serviceId + accountId`. `PortfolioIdentity` owns serialization and equality so widgets, tools, sockets, provider routes, and recorded orders do not infer account identity from labels or provider-local ids.
- Order writes belong to `/api/providers/trading/order` and `submitTradingOrder()`. Order history helpers are persistence infrastructure, not a public broker-write surface.
- Workflow execution is queue-first. API, published chat, builder, and child workflow execution all converge on `pendingExecution`, event writers, `/queue`, `/executions/[executionId]/stream`, and `/api/jobs/[jobId]` so cancellation, billing limits, stream output, and local Trigger.dev fallback stay consistent.
- Server-side workflow/tool calls carry short-lived internal JWT provenance. `InternalWorkflowExecutionContext` lets child workflow and tool calls prove parent workflow/block identity without reviving browser-session-dependent server calls.
- Copilot tool names now encode stable behavior. `read_workflow`, `read_workflow_logs`, `read_credentials`, `read_environment_variables`, `get_available_blocks`, and `get_agent_accessory_catalog` are registry-owned contracts, with server/client execution selected by metadata.
- Workflow editor state remains sub-block driven. Serialization, deploy previews, read-only panels, trigger hydration, and tool-param generation reuse common sub-block helpers instead of maintaining parallel preview registries or block-specific fallback paths.
